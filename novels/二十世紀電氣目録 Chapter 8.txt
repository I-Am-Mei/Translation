A chartered steamship, newly put into service this month, floated upon the surface of Lake Biwa.

—Why go to such great lengths to celebrate something as simple as registering a marriage?

Under Yōsuke’s direction, there had been no formal engagement gifts, no visits of greeting to either family. Perhaps because the gathering had originally been planned as a celebration for Yōsuke’s appointment as branch manager, no formalities such as mediators or ceremonial roles were observed. After a brief address from Yōsuke, the guests—men and women dressed casually in unadorned *kasuri* kimono or lightweight *ro* haori fastened with *himo* sashes—fell freely into idle chatter and began sampling the Western liquor and cuisine laid out on the tables.

Inako sat in her usual dark blue *kasuri* kimono, perched stiffly on a chair. She did not touch the food on the table, instead gazing listlessly around the deck. Most of the attendees were employees from the Mitsue Mizoe Shop’s Fushimi branch or workers from sake breweries affiliated with it. There was no sign of anyone from the main Mitsue family, not even Saki.

She was to become this man’s wife. She had made that decision, had steeled herself for it. Yet that resolve felt as flimsy as a stick thrust into mud.

The summer sun beat down mercilessly, yet strangely, she felt no heat. A suffocating tightness had gripped her since morning. This unpleasant tension reminded her of the moment before receiving an injection at the hospital.

"Well then, now that the mood’s warmed up, it’s time I gave this to you."

When she saw the pearl ring resting in Yōsuke’s open palm, every organ in her body recoiled in dread.

She had steeled herself. She had accepted living in hell.
